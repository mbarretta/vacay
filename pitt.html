Pittsburgh Getaway

Thursday, July 9 – Saturday, July 11

Downtown & World Cup

Zero Rideshares • Rain-Friendly

Today is all about keeping things walkable and weather-smart. We're leveraging the iconic incline to drop right into the heart of Downtown. You'll spend the day completely on foot, ducking into deeply historic indoor spaces to avoid the rain, catching the World Cup surrounded by local energy, and capping off the night with world-class jazz in a sophisticated, moody setting.

Start your morning by stepping back in time. After riding the 19th-century Monongahela Incline down Mount Washington, you'll cross the lenticular truss Smithfield Street Bridge into Downtown. La Prima brings true old-world Italian coffee culture to the city center. It’s cozy, unpretentious, and pours the perfect organic espresso to wake you up slowly on a rainy morning.

Before the afternoon match, take a flat, scenic walk to the very tip of the Golden Triangle. Tucked into Point State Park, this museum offers a deep, sheltered dive into the city's rugged frontier roots and its pivotal role in the French and Indian War. It's the perfect indoor escape that surrounds you with history while keeping you dry.

When 4:00 PM hits, Market Square is the place to be. This isn't just a basic sports bar; it's a massive, contemporary American pub boasting 15 state-of-the-art HDTVs and an incredible 90 craft beers on tap. Expect a lively, roaring atmosphere perfect for the World Cup, paired with elevated, hearty pub fare like truffle mushroom burgers and smoked wings.

Strip District Deep Dive

1 Morning Rideshare • Rain Refuge

Today requires just one morning rideshare to drop you into the bustling, historic heart of the Strip District. We are leaning heavily into the neighborhood's deep charm and personality. You'll navigate old-school European markets, explore a massive indoor museum to escape the heaviest rain, and enjoy an intimate, highly curated European dinner without a stiff white tablecloth in sight.

This is a legendary local institution that perfectly captures the unpretentious, highly satisfying culinary soul of the Strip. Known for their life-changing Blueberry Ricotta Pancakes and phenomenal House-Smoked Brisket Hash, it’s the ultimate comfort food start to the day. If there's a short wait, simply grab an espresso at the original La Prima counter next door and soak in the sidewalk market energy.

North Side Parks & Art

Free Light Rail • Sunny Outdoors

The skies are finally clearing, so today is about soaking up the sun and exploring the vibrant North Side. Using the free light rail system, you'll completely avoid the heavy weekend crowds and rideshare costs. Expect spectacular 19th-century architecture, world-renowned pop culture, and a cozy, upmarket wood-fired dinner to close out the getaway.

Take the free "T" to the North Side and take advantage of the beautiful weather by strolling through the oldest park in the city. You'll walk under spectacular mature tree canopies, past 19th-century landscaping, and alongside the meticulously preserved Victorian row houses of the Mexican War Streets. It’s a deeply peaceful route that feels a world away from the downtown hustle.
